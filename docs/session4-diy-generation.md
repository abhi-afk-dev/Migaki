# 🌿 Glass Bottle Terrarium
*Turn waste into a living ecosystem on your windowsill*

---

## Materials Needed

**From the bottle:**
- Clean glass bottle
- Bottle cap (if intact, for decorative top)
- Bottle label (removed cleanly — can be repurposed as jar tag)

**Additional materials:**
- Small river stones or pebbles (2-3 handfuls)
- Activated charcoal (1-2 tbsp)
- Potting soil (1-2 cups)
- Small indoor plants (succulents, fittonia, moss, or air plants)
- Decorative elements (optional): miniature figurines, colored glass pieces

---

## Tools Required

| Item | For Use |
|------|---------|
| Long spoon or chopsticks | For layering and positioning inside bottle |
- Water spray bottle | To mist soils and plants |
- Tweezers | To handle small plants |
- Gloves | Safety when handling glass shards (if any) |
- Newspaper/drop cloth | To protect workspace |

---

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. ⚠️ Safety First
- Wash the bottle thoroughly with warm soapy water, rinse well
- Inspect for cracks or sharp edges — **do not use if damaged**
- Dry completely (moisture = rot)

### 2. Prepare the Bottle
- Remove any label and residue (use warm water + baking soda if sticky)
- Dry the inside completely with a cloth or air dry

### 3. Creating the Drainage Layer (Foundation)
- Add river stones/pebbles to the bottom (1-2 inch depth)
- This prevents water accumulation and root rot

### 4. Add the Filter
- Sprinkle activated charcoal evenly over stones
- This keeps the air fresh and prevents odors/mold

### 5. Add Soil Layer
- Add potting soil (2-3 inch depth)
- Leave space at the top for plants

### 6. Plant Insertion
- Use tweezers or a long spoon to gently place plants
- Space them so they have room to grow
- Lightly tamp soil around each plant's base

### 7. Finishing Touches
- Add decorative elements (small stones, figurines)
- Spray the soil lightly to moisten it
- Replace cap if desired (can be painted or decorated)

### 8. Placement & Care
- Place near indirect sunlight (avoid harsh direct sun)
- Mist with water spray bottle every 7-10 days
- Open cap occasionally for fresh air circulation

## 🔄 Two Alternative Uses for the Same Bottle

### Option A: DIY Bottle Organizer
Perfect for bathroom, desk, or kitchen organization.

- Cut bottle in half (use glass cutter or score + hot water + cold water method)
- Sand smooth edges thoroughly
- Use bottom half as:
  - Toothbrush holder
  - Pen/pencil organizer
  - Makeup brush caddy
- Paint or decorate to match your decor

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ | Time: 15-20 minutes

---

### Option B: Rustic Lamp Base
Create a cozy lighting statement piece.

- Obtain string light battery pack or small LED light mechanism
- Thread lights through bottle neck
- Secure wire using a cork or wooden plug
- Add a natural rope or twine wrap around the bottle neck
- Use a small wooden slice as base (optional)
- **Note:** Ensure all electrical components are safe before using

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ | Time: 20-30 minutes
