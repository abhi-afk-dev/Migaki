**Re: Illegal Dumping and Health Hazard Near Lokhandwala Park, Andheri West**

**ISSUE SUMMARY**

We, the undersigned 47 residents of neighborhoods adjacent to Lokhandwala Park, Andheri West, Mumbai, bring to your urgent attention the ongoing illegal waste dumping that has been accumulating for over three weeks at [specific location near park]. This unattended waste has created severe public health and environmental hazards, directly violating the Solid Waste Management (SWM) Rules, 2026.

**CURRENT SITUATION**

- **Duration of accumulation:** More than 3 weeks
- **Hazards observed:**
  - Persistent foul smell affecting air quality
  - Stray animals (dogs, rats, birds) scavenging through garbage
  - Risk of disease spread to nearby residents
  - Breeding ground for mosquitoes and pests
  - Obstruction to public walkways around the park

**LEGAL VIOLATIONS CITED**

We assert that this situation contravenes multiple provisions of the **Solid Waste Management Rules, 2026**:

1. **Section 3(2)(e — Non-compliance by waste generator or agency**

   If a waste generator or agency fails to comply with any provision of these rules, the appropriate authority shall take action as prescribed under Section 15.

2. **Chapter II — General Duty of Local Authority**

   It shall be the duty of the local authority to ensure that:
   - (a) No waste is dumped in any public place except at designated sites.
   - (b) Waste collected from public places is disposed of in an environmentally sound manner.

3. **Section 4.1 — Prohibition of Littering**

   No waste generator or waste-collecting agency shall litter or allow littering at any public place.

4. **Section 11 — Illegal Dumping**

   Any illegal dumping of waste at public places attracts penalties as specified under Schedule II of these Rules.

5. **Section 12 — Failure to Clear Waste**

   If waste is not cleared within Twenty-Four hours of being reported or observed, the waste generator or local authority shall be deemed to be in contravention of Section 3(2).

**SPECIFIC DEMANDS**

We respectfully request the following immediate actions:

1. **Immediate removal** of all accumulated waste from the location within **72 hours** of receipt of this email (by **31 March 2026**).

2. Installation of **clear signage** at the site stating "No Dumping — Penalty Under SWM Rules 2026" in Hindi, Marathi, and English.

3. Deployment ofregular supervision to prevent recurrence, and implementation of monitoring strategies such as CCTV or frequent patrols.

4. **Action against the perpetrator(s)** responsible for the illegal dumping, as provided under Schedule II of SWM Rules 2026.

5. **Written confirmation** to [community.email@example.com] once cleanup is completed, including:
   - Date and time of cleanup
   - Quantity of waste removed (tonnage, if available)
   - Disposal method used
   - Preventive measures being instituted

**ESCALATION PATHWAY**

In the event that no written response or visible action is taken within 72 hours, we will initiate the following escalation measures:

1. **First escalation (96 hours, if no response):** Formal petition to the Municipal Commissioner of Greater Mumbai with copies to the State Environment Ministry.

2. **Second escalation (120 hours, if unresolved):** Public awareness campaign on social media and local media, with photographic evidence of the BMC inaction.

3. **Third escalation (no response within 168 hours):** Filing of a Public Interest Litigation (PIL) before the Hon’ble Bombay High Court seeking judicial intervention under Article 226 of the Constitution for civic negligence and violation of SWM Rules 2026.

**COMMUNITY SIGNATORIES**

We attach the signatures of 47 residents in support of this complaint. These residents include: seniors, parents with young children, local shop owners, and students. The collective voice of the community demands urgent action.

**CONCLUSION**

We believe in constructive civic engagement. The residents have documented evidence (attached) of the dumping site and its progression over the past three weeks. We look forward to prompt action and a cleaner, healthier environment.
